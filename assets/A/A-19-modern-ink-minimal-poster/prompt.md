# A-19 · Modern Ink Minimal Poster

Scene:
A vertical 9:16 luxury poster in a modern Chinese ink-wash aesthetic. The composition is quiet, spacious, and premium: approximately 60 percent of the image is intentional blank mist and pale paper texture. In the lower half, a mirror-still lake sits between steep, strange mountain silhouettes. The mountains are sharp and elegant, like a fusion of Song dynasty landscape painting and contemporary architectural minimalism. A thin layer of spring morning mist drifts across the waterline.

Subject:
The main subject is the landscape itself: jagged distant peaks, a central calm lake, and one tiny pavilion silhouette near the lower third to provide scale. The pavilion is minimal, almost a single brush mark, with no people visible. The lake reflects the mountain shapes softly, but the reflection is lighter and blurred like diluted ink.

Important details:
Use a restrained spring palette: pale celadon green, mist blue, diluted black ink, warm off-white paper, and light grey. Add subtle mineral-pigment granulation in the green areas and soft feathered ink edges along the mountains. Keep the image uncluttered. The negative space must feel expensive and deliberate, suitable for a tea brand, cultural real estate campaign, or museum gift-shop poster. Add no readable text, no logo, no watermark, and no border.

Use case:
High-end editorial / brand poster for modern Chinese culture, tea, architecture, wellness, or cultural tourism. It should feel like a finished poster artwork that could hang in a boutique hotel lobby or premium tea house.

Constraints:
Single image only. Aspect ratio 9:16 vertical. No typography. No calligraphy. No seals. No extra symbols. No people. No birds. No boats. No sun disk. No harsh contrast. No saturated colors. Avoid generic fantasy mountains; keep it elegant, sparse, atmospheric, and materially close to ink on fine paper.
