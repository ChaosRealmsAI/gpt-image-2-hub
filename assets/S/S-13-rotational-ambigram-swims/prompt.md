# S-13 · Rotational Ambigram SWIMS

Scene:
A square luxury typography specimen poster on deep matte black velvet. The entire image is built around one central 180-degree rotational ambigram wordmark. The composition is symmetrical, centered, and minimal, like a high-end tattoo flash sheet or jewelry brand emblem photographed in a dark studio.

Subject:
Create a single ornate gothic calligraphy ambigram. The central word must read exactly "SWIMS" when viewed normally. When the entire image is rotated 180 degrees upside down, the same central wordmark must still read exactly "SWIMS". Engineer the letters as reversible glyph pairs: the first S rotates into the final S, W rotates into M, I stays I, M rotates into W, and the final S rotates into the first S. The design should feel like one continuous hand-lettered blackletter logo, not mirrored duplicate text.

Important details:
Use burnished metallic gold lettering with subtle bevels, engraved shadow lines, and tiny highlights on the stroke edges. The strokes should be thick enough for readability, with elegant blackletter flourishes that hide the geometric transformation. Keep the baseline perfectly horizontal and keep the word centered. Add a faint circular construction guide behind the wordmark, barely visible in dark grey, suggesting typographic engineering.

Use case:
Tattoo design, ring engraving, gothic logo mark, typography challenge showcase, and viral demonstration of gpt-image-2 text-shape reasoning.

Constraints:
Single image only. Aspect ratio 1:1. The only readable text in the image must be the central ambigram. No captions, no labels, no arrows, no watermark, no signature, no extra words. Do not show a second rotated copy. Do not split the image into panels. Prioritize exact readability: upright reads "SWIMS"; upside-down 180-degree rotation also reads "SWIMS".
